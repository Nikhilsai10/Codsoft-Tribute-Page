Virat Kohli

Living Legend


Virat Kohli was born on 5 November 1988 in Delhi to Prem Kohli and Saroj Kohli’s home. His wife’s name is Anushka Sharma. Kohli’s childhood was spent in the Uttam Nagar area of Delhi and he studied at Vishal Bharti Public School.

He is also often called ‘Chiku’. This nickname has been given to Virat by his coach Ajeet Chaudhary.

Kohli started his cricket career in 1998 by joining the West Delhi Cricket Association. He was selected in under-19 cricket in July 2006 and in November 2006 he played his first Ranji match against Tamil Nadu for Delhi.

Kohli’s story is very famous that his father Prem Kohli died on 18 December 2006 and the next day Virat played brilliant innings of 90 runs against Karnataka.

In the 2008 Under-19 World Cup, Virat Kohli captained the Indian team and also won the World Cup in a great way. Ravindra Jadeja was also in his team at that time.)

When Kohli was the first bid in the IPL of 2008, Bangalore bought him for around Rs 12 lakh. While in the coming 2018, their rate in the IPL was Rs 17 crore. Meaning, 141 times more in just 10 years.

He played the first international ODI of his career in 2008 against Sri Lanka and the first T20 match against Zimbabwe in 2010 and the first Test match in 2011 against West Indies.

In 2017, Forbes magazine named Virat Kohli as India’s most valuable celebrity brand. In 2018, Forbes magazine ranked Virat 83rd in the list of top-100 highest-grossing players. He is the only Indian player to get a place on this list.

In March 2013, Virat Kohli started a charity called the Virat Kohli Foundation. This charity works for poor children and their education.

Virat Kohli was awarded the Padma Shri, India’s fourth-highest honor in 2013 and the Arjuna Award in 2013 itself. And in 2018, Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna, India’s biggest sports award, was also awarded.

He is the only Indian player to score a century in the first match of the World Cup. He set this record against Bangladesh in the 2011 World Cup.

The record for the fastest 50 centuries in international cricket is named after two players, Hashim Amla and Virat Kohli. Both of them made this record 348 matches of the game.

Kohli is fond of tattoos. He has got tattoos done four times. He loves samurai warrior tattoos the most. He is also known for his fashion sense. His name is among the 10 best-dressed men.
